project 3 (LIA: Learning Integrated Assessment)

LIA DropBox (One Drive) link

Complete a project of your choice using some of what you have learned this semester in Fusion & Blender, but could also be from other classes.

You can work alone or in a group.

You are welcome to do a brand new project or refine and extend an existing project.

  • design a project to be 3d printed
  • tell a story using Blender
  • create a character in Blender
  • create a "level" (scene or setting in Blender)
  • 3d print characters to film in stop motion
  • combine photos / video in Blender to create something cool
  • illustrate a background to incorporate into a 3d Blender scene

Here are some more suggestions from CoPilot (AI)

  • Animated Short Film: Create a short film that tells a story using animated characters and environments.
  • 3D Character Design: Design and model a detailed 3D character, complete with textures and rigging for animation.
  • Architectural Visualization: Model a realistic building or interior space, focusing on lighting and materials to create a photorealistic render.
  • Product Visualization: Create a 3D model of a product, such as a piece of furniture or a gadget, and render it in a realistic setting.
  • Game Asset Creation: Design and model assets for a video game, such as characters, vehicles, or environments.
  • Motion Graphics: Develop a motion graphics sequence for a title or logo, incorporating text and animated elements.
  • Virtual Reality Environment: Create an immersive VR environment that users can explore, focusing on interactivity and realism.
  • Special Effects Scene: Simulate a special effects scene, such as an explosion, water simulation, or smoke, using Blender's physics and particle systems.
  • 3D Printed Object: Design a 3D model that can be printed using a 3D printer, considering the constraints and possibilities of 3D printing technology.
  • Abstract Art: Experiment with Blender's tools to create an abstract piece of 3D art, focusing on form, color, and composition.

deliverables

  • 1-paragraph description / plan / story brief (10%)
  • visual plan / moodboard / sketches / storyboard (20%)
  • performance or presentation of your work (50%)
  • post project to your site including the above 3 elements (20%)

project 2: tell a story, in 3d, using Blender

In a joint project with both sections and 3rd-year students, you will create a scene that tells a short story.

Your scene should include a clear beginning, middle and end.

  • beginning: an introduction of the main problem or tension that needs to be resolved
  • middle: develop the story with rising action
  • end: a climax where the tension is resolved, the problem is solved
  • bonus: denouement, a final explanation or summary of any unresolved strands of the plot

Your scene should be made up of

  • a general background or landscape, i.e. an urban environment, or on a lake, or on the moon
  • objects such as cars, boats / fish, or aliens
  • subjects such as people, characters, monsters, cute blocks with faces

deliverables

  • a 1-paragraph story brief
  • a storyboard with 3 or more panels
  • a gallery of images of all assets / elements in your scene
  • video (uploaded to YouTube) of your final project / story

Keep it simple, at least to start. If you have time, add more elements, characters, a plot twist etc. Think 6-10 seconds
